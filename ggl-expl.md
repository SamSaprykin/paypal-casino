## GGL Blocked Words & Compliance Rules in Germany

In Germany, the **Gemeinsame Glücksspielbehörde der Länder (GGL)** enforces strict regulations on language and advertising for gambling operators and affiliates. "Blocked words" generally refer to terms and phrases prohibited by the State Treaty on Gambling (**GlüStV 2021**) in order to protect players and to clearly distinguish between different types of gambling.

---

### 1. **Prohibited Terminology (Advertising Bans)**

Providers of virtual slot machines (_Online Slots_) are **strictly forbidden** from using wording that implies a "classic" casino experience—such as references to table games (Roulette, Blackjack, etc.). Key restrictions include:

- **"Casino" / "Online Casino"**: These terms must **not** be used for virtual slot providers.  
  **Approved alternatives:**  
  - _Spielhalle_ (Gaming Hall)  
  - _Spielothek_  
  - _Automatenspiel_ (Slot Games)
  
- **"Jackpot"**: _Any_ advertising for jackpots on virtual slot machines is entirely banned to prevent excessive incentivization.

- **"Live"**: The word "Live" (as in live games or live dealers) is regulated separately by the German federal states and must **not** be used by slot providers.

---

### 2. **Forbidden Marketing Phrases**

The GGL also bans types of language in marketing that could mislead consumers or encourage addictive behaviors:

- **Financial Myths:**  
  - No phrases suggesting gambling can solve financial problems or function as a substitute for work.
- **Skill-Based Claims:**  
  - Claims that imply players can influence outcomes by “strategy” or “skill” (slots are chance-based only).
- **Pressure Language:**  
  - No urging players to play longer, wager more, or reinvest their winnings directly.
- **Youth-Oriented Language:**  
  - Slang or messaging targeting minors or young adults is strictly prohibited.

---

### 3. **Technical Blocking (IP & Payment)**

Beyond language, the GGL employs technical measures against unlicensed operators:

- **IP Blocking:**  
  - The GGL can instruct Internet Service Providers (ISPs) to block illegal gambling websites.
- **Payment Blocking:**  
  - Financial institutions may be directed to block deposits and withdrawals between German players and unlicensed/offshore sites.

---

### 4. **Compliance Summary Table**

If you are an operator or affiliate, **avoid the "C-word" ("Casino") completely unless legally permitted**; only use terms allowed under your specific license.

| Restricted Term          | Approved Alternative              |
|-------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Casino / Online Casino  | Online-Spielothek / Slot-Anbieter |
| Jackpot                 | _(No direct alternative in ads)_  |
| Casino Games            | Virtuelle Automatenspiele         |
